A Theorist Panel Discussion

This project was the final writing assignment for my Theory & Inquiry course at George Mason University. The objective of this project was to synthesize the perspectives of several theoretical texts we read and discussed in class throughout the semester by creating a dialogue where the authors of said theoretical texts would discuss a passage from another text. I thoroughly enjoyed this project because it presented the constant challenge of shifting my voice as a writer to effectively apply each theorist’s perspective in a realistic but compelling manner. Shifting one’s authorial voice to capture another voice is an essential skill for effective UX writers since their primary objective is to advocate for the user via their employer’s desired voice. Therefore, I view this project as a useful exercise for improving my voice versatility as a writer and UX professional.

Accessibility Report

I completed this project for a Technical Editing course I took at George Mason University. The task was to determine the accessibility of the front webpage of the Northern Virginia news platform InsideNova using WAVE, an internet accessibility evaluation tool. Utilizing the accessibility findings of WAVE, I argued for specific, actionable changes that would likely improve readers’ overall experience using the platform. I found this project to be a valuable exercise in grounding my writing in concrete research and analytic data albeit on a smaller scale. That being said, research and data-backed writing is an important skill for UX professionals to develop as it communicates the vital information they have learned about their users.

RYM Usability Test

The following section contains the exhaustive report on the usability test I conducted for the community feature of the website RateYourMusic.com as part of a mini project for a UX Design class I took at George Mason University. Based on my initial observations about the community feature, I created a series of tasks for participants to execute and follow-up questions for them to answer. These tasks and questions were grouped into three distinct UX-centric categories once again based on my personal takeaways about the experience quality of the website’s community feature. The five participants’ responses and feedback were then recorded and analyzed in this report.
