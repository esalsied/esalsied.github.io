Stolid Soul

I caught glimpses of his face before,
But my view was never clear.
Now it is.

His face bleaches in
Around eggshell eyes.
The result is a cold contrast,
Human in theory.

Restraint comes to mind upon viewing.
A restraint permanently fixed
In a stolid, stagnant soul.

The thick brush atop weathered features
Contorts itself in bizarre sweeps.
Aimless, it resigns to a solitary crevice
Yet remains in plain view.

His lips are the greatest imitator
But become stubborn on opposing ends
When smiling is in order.

Now that my view
Is clear in brief glimpses,
My hope returns as a lie.
